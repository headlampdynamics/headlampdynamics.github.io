Core Solutions

Control Logic & Dynamic Tuning

Sharper setpoints, smoother recovery, less second-guessing.

  • Identify dynamic bottlenecks and sensitivity in real process data.
  • Retune control code (e.g. Allen-Bradley, DCS) using system behavior—not guesses.
  • Use LTC and model-based logic to stabilize without overcorrection.

Outcome: tighter control with fewer alarms and smoother transitions.

Dynamic Monitoring & Fault Signals

Catch drift, instability, and subtle changes before they escalate.

  • Model how your system slows down or shifts near failure points.
  • Generate early-warning signals from dynamics, not just thresholds.
  • Deploy compact, interpretable models in Python or MATLAB.

Outcome: earlier intervention, better root-cause insight, and fewer blind spots.

Are We Right for You?

You're responsible for keeping a process running reliably—one where downtime costs money, variability eats margins, and small issues can spiral into bigger ones if they aren't caught early. Maybe it's in indoor ag, brewing, wastewater, or batch manufacturing. The setup works, but could we make it work better?

Our focus is on the dynamics—how your system behaves over time, under load, and during change. We don't just look at average performance; we look at how things shift, stabilize, and recover. If your system is slow to respond, hard to tune, or showing patterns you can’t quite explain, that’s where we come in.

We build lightweight models that capture how your process actually behaves—not just under ideal conditions, but in the real operating envelope. That means fewer assumptions, clearer insight, and better decisions when something goes off script.

We specialize in tuning control logic, isolating root causes of variability, and surfacing subtle faults early—so your team can act before problems grow. Our methods are built for real-world data, constrained budgets, and the need to justify every fix.

If economic conditions make capital upgrades hard to justify, we offer a way to get more out of the systems you already have. The goal: fewer surprises, faster response, and measurable gains in efficiency and uptime.
